# Amazon Leadership Principles — Intern Interview Prep

## How Amazon Behavioral Interviews Work

- Every question maps to a **Leadership Principle (LP)**
- Each interviewer is assigned 2-3 LPs to assess
- For **internships**, expect 1-2 behavioral rounds (sometimes mixed with coding)
- They want **real examples**, never hypothetical answers

---

## The STAR Method

Use this framework for **every** answer:

| Step | What | Time |
|------|------|------|
| **S** — Situation | Set the scene (1-2 sentences, brief context) | 10% |
| **T** — Task | What was YOUR responsibility? | 10% |
| **A** — Action | What did YOU specifically do? Be detailed. | **60%** |
| **R** — Result | What was the outcome? Quantify if possible. | 20% |

### Golden Rules

1. Always say **"I"**, not "we" — they want YOUR contributions
2. Be **specific** — dates, numbers, metrics, team sizes
3. Prepare **8-10 stories** that cover multiple LPs each
4. Have at least **1 failure/conflict story** ready
5. Keep answers to **2-3 minutes**
6. "Tell me about a time..." = they want a REAL example

---

## Priority LPs for Interns

Focus your prep on these 8 first — they come up the most:

1. **Customer Obsession** — almost always asked
2. **Ownership** — very common
3. **Learn and Be Curious** — huge for interns
4. **Bias for Action** — common
5. **Earn Trust** — common (conflict stories)
6. **Deliver Results** — common
7. **Dive Deep** — technical depth
8. **Insist on the Highest Standards** — quality focus

---

## All 16 LPs with Common Questions

### 1. Customer Obsession ⭐⭐⭐

> *"Leaders start with the customer and work backwards."*

**What they're looking for:** You think about end users, not just code. You prioritize customer impact over internal convenience.

**For interns:** Think of users of your projects, classmates using your tool, or anyone you've "served" with your work.

- Tell me about a time you had to prioritize the needs of a customer/user.
- Describe a project where you went above and beyond for the end user.
- Tell me about a time you received negative feedback from a user. What did you do?
- How did you determine what your customer/user really needed vs. what they asked for?

---

### 2. Ownership ⭐⭐⭐

> *"Leaders never say 'that's not my job.' They act on behalf of the entire company."*

**What they're looking for:** You take initiative without being asked. You don't let problems fester because "it's someone else's area." You think long-term.

- Tell me about a time you took on something outside your area of responsibility.
- Describe a time you saw a problem and took the initiative to fix it.
- Tell me about a time you had to make a tradeoff between short-term and long-term goals.
- Give an example of when you took ownership of a project that was struggling.

---

### 3. Invent and Simplify ⭐⭐

> *"Leaders expect and require innovation and always find ways to simplify."*

**For interns:** Course projects where you found a creative solution, automated something tedious, or simplified a complex process.

- Tell me about a time you found a simple solution to a complex problem.
- Describe an innovative idea you had. How did you implement it?
- Tell me about a time you simplified a process or system.
- Give an example of when you challenged the status quo.

---

### 4. Are Right, A Lot ⭐⭐

> *"Leaders have strong judgment and good instincts. They seek diverse perspectives and work to disconfirm their beliefs."*

**What they're looking for:** Good decision-making process (not just good outcomes). You seek input from others. You can change your mind with new data.

- Tell me about a time you made a decision with incomplete information.
- Describe a time when you were wrong. How did you handle it?
- Tell me about a time you had to make a quick decision under pressure.
- Give an example of when you used data to make a decision.

---

### 5. Learn and Be Curious ⭐⭐⭐

> *"Leaders are never done learning and always seek to improve themselves."*

**For interns:** This is HUGE. They want to see you're a fast learner who's genuinely curious about technology.

- Tell me about a time you had to learn something quickly to complete a project.
- Describe a skill you taught yourself. Why and how?
- Tell me about a time your curiosity led you to explore something new.
- What's the most recent thing you learned? How did you learn it?

---

### 6. Hire and Develop the Best

> *"Leaders raise the performance bar with every hire and promotion."*

Less common for interns, but can come up as "mentoring" or "teamwork."

- Tell me about a time you mentored or helped a teammate grow.
- Describe a time you gave someone constructive feedback.
- How do you help bring out the best in people you work with?

---

### 7. Insist on the Highest Standards ⭐⭐

> *"Leaders have relentlessly high standards."*

**For interns:** Times you pushed for quality code, better testing, or refused to ship something half-baked.

- Tell me about a time you refused to compromise on quality.
- Describe a time when you raised the bar for your team or project.
- Tell me about a time you caught a mistake that others missed.
- How do you ensure quality in your work?

---

### 8. Think Big

> *"Thinking small is a self-fulfilling prophecy."*

- Tell me about your most ambitious project.
- Describe a time you proposed a bold idea.
- How do you decide what to work on? How do you prioritize impact?

---

### 9. Bias for Action ⭐⭐⭐

> *"Speed matters in business. Many decisions are reversible and do not need extensive study."*

**What they're looking for:** You don't get paralyzed by analysis. You take calculated risks. You act quickly when it matters.

- Tell me about a time you had to act quickly without having all the information.
- Describe a time when you took a calculated risk.
- Tell me about a time you had to make a decision without your manager's input.
- Give an example of when waiting would have made things worse.

---

### 10. Frugality

> *"Accomplish more with less."*

- Tell me about a time you accomplished something with limited resources.
- Describe a time you found a cost-effective solution.
- How do you prioritize when you can't do everything?

---

### 11. Earn Trust ⭐⭐⭐

> *"Leaders listen attentively, speak candidly, and treat others respectfully."*

**What they're looking for:** You're honest even when it's uncomfortable. You admit mistakes. You handle disagreements respectfully.

- Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a teammate. How did you resolve it?
- Describe a time you had to deliver difficult/uncomfortable feedback.
- Tell me about a time you admitted a mistake to your team.
- How do you build trust with people you work with?

---

### 12. Dive Deep ⭐⭐

> *"Leaders operate at all levels, stay connected to details, and audit frequently."*

**For interns:** Debugging stories, investigating root causes, reading source code to understand something deeply.

- Tell me about a time you had to debug a difficult issue. Walk me through your process.
- Describe a time you needed to understand a system deeply to solve a problem.
- Tell me about a time when the details really mattered.
- Give an example of when surface-level analysis wasn't enough.

---

### 13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit ⭐⭐

> *"Leaders respectfully challenge decisions when they disagree. Once a decision is made, they commit wholly."*

- Tell me about a time you disagreed with your team or manager.
- Describe a time you pushed back on a decision. What happened?
- Tell me about a time you had to commit to a decision you disagreed with.
- How do you handle situations where you strongly disagree with the direction?

---

### 14. Deliver Results ⭐⭐⭐

> *"Leaders focus on the key inputs and deliver them with the right quality and in a timely fashion."*

**What they're looking for:** You finish what you start. You overcome obstacles. You deliver on time.

- Tell me about your most impactful project. What results did you achieve?
- Describe a time you had a tight deadline. How did you ensure delivery?
- Tell me about a time you had to overcome significant obstacles to deliver.
- Give an example of a goal you set and achieved. How did you measure success?

---

### 15. Strive to Be Earth's Best Employer

- Tell me about a time you helped create a positive team environment.
- Describe a time you supported a struggling teammate.

---

### 16. Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility

- Tell me about a time you considered the broader impact of your work.
- How do you think about the ethical implications of technology?

---

## Sample STAR Answer

**Question:** "Tell me about a time you had to learn something quickly."
**LP:** Learn and Be Curious

> **S** — During my sophomore year, I joined a hackathon where my team decided to build a real-time chat app, but none of us knew WebSockets.
>
> **T** — I was responsible for the backend. I had 36 hours to learn WebSocket programming and build a working server.
>
> **A** — I spent the first 3 hours reading the Socket.IO documentation and building a minimal echo server. Then I found a tutorial that built a simple chat room, which I worked through line by line. By hour 6, I had a working prototype. I broke the remaining work into milestones: (1) multi-room support, (2) message persistence with MongoDB, (3) typing indicators. I hit each one ahead of schedule because the initial deep-dive gave me a solid foundation.
>
> **R** — We had a fully working app by the deadline and won 2nd place out of 30 teams. The judge specifically praised the real-time features. I continued using WebSockets in two later projects, so the investment paid off long-term.

**Why this works:**
- Specific (hackathon, Socket.IO, MongoDB, 36 hours, 30 teams)
- "I" focused (not "we learned")
- Shows the learning PROCESS, not just the outcome
- Quantified result (2nd place out of 30)
- Long-term impact mentioned
